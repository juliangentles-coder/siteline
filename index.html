How these were measured

  • Aerial video captured over public streets, framed at an oblique angle — steep enough to separate objects, shallow enough for the detector to recognise them.
  • Objects detected and tracked frame to frame, so each subject is counted once as it crosses the line. The line coordinates are printed on every card.
  • People and vehicles are counted in aggregate only. No faces, no plates, no identification of anyone.
  • Confidence is the detector's mean score across the clip. Above 0.5 is usable; lower means objects were small or partly hidden and the count runs conservative.

What these numbers are not

  • Not multi-day averages. Each is a single sample of two to three minutes, projected to an hour for comparison.
  • Not certified counts. Good estimates for a decision, not court-defensible traffic studies.
  • Not directly comparable between sites without care — framing, angle and time of day differ. Same-site comparisons over time are the reliable ones.
  • Dense crowds run under, not over: overlapping people merge and go uncounted.
